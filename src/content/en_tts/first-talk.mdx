[gentle, soft, contemplative]

At Varanasi, in the deer park at Isipatana, the buddha addressed the five monks: [long pause]

"If you've left ordinary life behind to find the truth, [pause] there are two dead ends you shouldn't waste yourself on. [long pause]

first, Chasing pleasure — [pause] it's cheap, [pause] shallow, [pause] and gets you nowhere. [pause]

second Punishing yourself — [pause] it's painful, [pause] pointless, [pause] and also gets you nowhere. [long pause]

Steering clear of both of those, [pause] I've found a path that runs down the middle. [pause] It clears your sight and settles your mind, [pause] and it leads to calm, [pause] real understanding, [pause] and to true freedom. [long pause]

And what is that middle path that clears your sight and settles your mind — [pause] that leads to calm, real understanding, and true freedom? [pause] It's this — [pause] eight things to get right: [long pause]

Seeing clearly. [pause]
Living with intention. [pause]
Speaking honestly. [pause]
Acting decently. [pause]
Earning a living that does no harm. [pause]
Working steadily. [pause]
Staying mindful. [pause]
And focusing deeply. [long pause]

That's the middle path I found — [pause] the one that clears your sight and settles your mind, [pause] and leads to a calm, real understanding, and true freedom. [long pause]

(These are often called the Noble Eightfold Path: [pause] Right View, [pause] Right Intention, [pause] Right Speech, [pause] Right Action, [pause] Right Livelihood, [pause] Right Effort, [pause] Right Mindfulness, [pause] Right Concentration.) [long pause]

---

## The Four Noble Truths

[gentle, contemplative]

Suffering. [long pause] Being born is hard. [pause] Growing old is hard. [pause] Getting sick is hard. [pause] Dying is hard. [pause] Being stuck with what you can't stand hurts; [pause] being torn from what you love hurts; [pause] not getting what you want hurts. [pause] The whole bundle of grasping at life — [pause] that's suffering. [long pause]

Where it comes from. [pause] It comes from craving — [pause] the restless wanting that keeps pulling you back for more, [pause] hunting for the next good feeling wherever you can find one: [pause] wanting pleasure, [pause] wanting to keep existing, [pause] wanting to stop existing. [long pause]

How it ends. [pause] It's the complete fading-out of that very craving — [pause] finally letting it go, [pause] releasing it, [pause] holding on to none of it. [long pause]

The way there. [pause] It's simply this same eightfold path: [pause] seeing clearly, [pause] living with intention, [pause] speaking honestly, [pause] acting decently, [pause] earning a living that does no harm, [pause] working steadily, [pause] staying mindful, [pause] and focusing deeply. [long pause]

---

## Knowing Each One Three Ways

[gentle, contemplative]

For each of these four truths, [pause] my understanding deepened in three steps. [pause] Take suffering: [long pause]

First I saw clearly: [pause] *this is suffering.* [pause]
Then I understood: [pause] *this is something to be fully grasped.* [pause]
Then I knew: [pause] *I have fully grasped it.* [long pause]

The same three steps applied to all four: [long pause]

Suffering — [pause] recognize it; [pause] understand it; [pause] I've known it. [pause]
Its cause — [pause] recognize it; [pause] let it go; [pause] I've freed myself. [pause]
Its ending — [pause] recognize it; [pause] experience it; [pause] I've lived it. [pause]
The path — [pause] recognize it; [pause] develop it; [pause] I've made it mine. [long pause]

---

As long as my understanding of these four truths — [pause] in all three steps, [pause] across all twelve points — [pause] wasn't completely clear, [pause] I didn't claim to be fully awakened. [pause] But once it *was* completely clear, [pause] then I knew I had woken up fully, [pause] with nothing left to do. [long pause] And the certainty settled in me: [pause] *My freedom can't be shaken. [pause] This is my last birth. [pause] There's nothing more after this.* [long pause]

---

## How They Heard It

[gentle, contemplative]

That's what the Buddha said, [pause] and the five of them were glad to hear it. [long pause]

And while he was speaking, [pause] something opened up in one of them, [pause] Kondañña — [pause] a clear, [pause] clean insight cut through: [long pause]

> *Anything that begins is something that ends.* [long pause]

And the moment the Buddha set this teaching in motion, [pause] the call went up. [long pause]

All of Earth's gods cried out: [pause] "Near Varanasi, [pause] in the deer park at Isipatana, [pause] the Buddha has set in motion the unsurpassed wheel of truth — [pause] and no one anywhere, [pause] no seeker or sage, [pause] no god, [pause] no demon, [pause] no one at all, [pause] can stop it from turning." [long pause]

And the moment the Buddha set this teaching going, [pause] word of it seemed to ripple outward and upward, [pause] further and further, [pause] as if the whole universe had felt it move. [long pause]

So in that moment, [pause] in that instant, [pause] the cry rose all the way to the highest heavens. [pause] The ten-thousandfold universe-system shook and shuddered and trembled, [pause] and a vast, [pause] boundless light broke out across it — [pause] brighter even than the radiance of the gods themselves. [long pause]

Then the Buddha spoke these words: [pause] **"Kondañña has understood! [pause] Kondañña has truly understood!"** [long pause]

And that's how Kondañña came to be called Aññā Kondañña — [pause] *Kondañña Who Knows.*
